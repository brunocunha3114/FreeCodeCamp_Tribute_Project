Ferdinand Magellan

Ferdinand Magellan (c. 1480 – 27 April 1521) was a Portuguese explorer who planned and led the 1519–22 Spanish expedition to the East Indies. His remarkable journey included:

  • Circumnavigation of Earth: Magellan's expedition achieved the first circumnavigation of Earth in history. His fleet sailed from Spain, crossed the Atlantic, and discovered the Strait of Magellan, allowing them to enter the Pacific Ocean. They then navigated across the Pacific to reach Asia.
  • Discovery of the Pacific Ocean: Magellan's voyage led to the naming of the Pacific Ocean, as it was the first time Europeans had sailed across it.
  • Tragic End: Magellan died in the Philippines during the expedition, but his crew completed the return trip to Spain in 1522.

His achievement remains a testament to human curiosity, courage, and determination in the Age of Exploration. Only 22 of the original 270 crew members survived the journey back to Europe.
